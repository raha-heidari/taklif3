Iranian customs

As a part of the rich culture of this land, Iranian customs and traditions reflect the history, art and social life of the Iranian people. These customs include various celebrations and ceremonies such as Nowruz, Yalda and Ashura, each of which have symbols of friendship, solidarity and respect for nature and history.
Iranians are known for their hospitality and on occasions and celebrations, they prepare traditional foods and local sweets with love and respect. Also, the language of poetry and literature plays an important role in their daily life, and the poems of great poets such as Hafez and Saadi have a place in people's hearts.

Iranian customs also include respect for elders, the value of family and preservation of local traditions, all of which form part of the cultural identity of this people.
Iranian culture and traditions not only shape our national identity but also represent our history, art and social life. By knowing these customs, we can understand the depth of Iran's rich culture.
